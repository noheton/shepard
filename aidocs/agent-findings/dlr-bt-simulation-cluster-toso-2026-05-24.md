# DLR-BT simulation cluster — "unsere Simulanten"

## 1. Who is Toso

The Toso the user refers to is **Dr. Nathalie Toso-Pentecôte**, who appears in
DLR-BT's publication record under both name forms — `Toso-Pentecote, Nathalie`
(older publications, e.g. eLib 129291, 2019) and `Toso, Nathalie` (current
authoritative byline since ~2020). She is **Head of the Structural Integrity
Department** at DLR's Institute of Structures and Design (Institut für Bauweisen
und Strukturtechnologie, DLR-BT) in Stuttgart-Vaihingen. The institute-internal
report identifier prefix on every publication of hers that is a DLR-IB —
`DLR-IB-BT-ST-YYYY-NNN` — confirms the **BT-ST** organisational unit: BT =
Bauweisen und Strukturtechnologie, ST = Strukturmechanik (Structural Integrity in
English).

She appears in **14 of the 20 most recent results** in the eLib "toso" query
(160 results total, 2009–2025), almost always in last-author or senior-author
position — the publication-record pattern of a department lead supervising the
people who do the bench work.

Two other Tosos sit in the same publication graph:

- **Toso, Yves** — junior engineer in the same BT-ST unit, ~10 publications
  including sole-author DLR-IB internal reports on material-card development
  (eLib 130893, 126538, 210707) and crashworthiness simulation (eLib 137391
  "SIMULATION OF THE IMPACT BEHAVIOR OF VARIOUS MATERIALS", Aerospace Europe
  Conf. 2020). Almost certainly a direct report of Nathalie's. Working from
  publication patterns alone he reads as the cluster's hands-on
  material-card-development engineer.
- **Toso, Federico** — appears once (eLib 206525, "Inflatable Heat Shields
  solutions for hypersonic re-entry"), affiliated with **Deimos Space** (Italian
  industry partner on the EU EFESTO-2 hypersonic re-entry project). Not part of
  the DLR-BT cluster — coincidental surname match in the search results.

**Identity collision note for the user:** when the conversation says "Toso"
generically, the high-prior reading is Nathalie. When discussing material-card
internal reports, it may be Yves. The two Tosos sit in the same team; both are
in scope for any DLR-BT simulation-cluster outreach.

## 3. Method landscape

**The dominant solver across the cluster is LS-DYNA.** Explicit mentions in
titles:

- eLib 216723 "...thick-walled composite hydrogen tanks under internal pressure
  and impact load **with LS-DYNA**"
- eLib 209961 "Drone and bird strike on a polycarbonate helicopter windshield"
  (17. **Deutsches LS-DYNA Forum** / Ansys German Innovation Conference)
- eLib 207501 "High-fidelity modelling of composite specimens manufactured with
  the automated fibre placement technique" (17. Deutsches LS-DYNA Forum)
- eLib 132044 ARENA2036 "digitaler Prototyp" — DYNAmore (Liebold, Haufe) as
  co-authors confirms LS-DYNA as the working environment

**Method clusters** visible across the 2015–2025 publication sweep:

1. **Explicit-dynamics impact / crashworthiness FEM** — composite crush,
   bird-strike, drone-strike, bolted-joint energy absorption, tyre-rubber impact,
   ditching. This is the cluster's dominant capability, all in LS-DYNA. Strain
   rates explicitly modelled from quasi-static through intermediate (~100 s⁻¹) to
   high (1000+ s⁻¹).
2. **Cryogenic / hydrogen-tank simulation** — coupled internal pressure +
   external impact for LH₂ tanks; close-to-cryogenic crush testing with matched
   simulation. Active research front (2024–2025 surge). The recurring chain
   Waimer + Vinot + Behling + Toso = the LH₂ tank sub-team.
3. **AFP defect modelling** — high-fidelity FEM of automated-fibre-placement
   process defects; mesh-resolved gap/overlap modelling. Yoo + Vinot + Toso.
   Directly relevant to the MFFD use case Shepard already ships in the
   `mffd-showcase` seed.
4. **Material-card development** — under-the-hood feeder for everything else.
   Dynamic tension/compression/shear at temperature, fracture-toughness
   campaigns, GAN-based data augmentation of small material-test datasets
   (eLib 216903, 2025). Yves Toso's DLR-IB reports are pure material-card
   documentation.
5. **Data-driven / ML-augmented simulation** — Constitutive Artificial Neural
   Networks (CANN) for strain-rate prediction (eLib 216899), GAN data
   augmentation for material testing (eLib 216903), both 2025. The cluster is
   actively bringing ML into the constitutive-modelling layer.

## 4. Data-management implications for shepard

The cluster's typical work-product life-cycle: **material test → fitted
material card → input-deck assembly → parameter sweep → result post-processing
→ validation against companion physical test → publication**. Each stage
maps cleanly onto shepard primitives, but several mappings need slight
sharpening of the existing model.

### (a) Input-deck versioning — `FileReference` + `version` annotation, mostly there

LS-DYNA `.k` keyword decks, `*INCLUDE` chains, mesh files (`.k`, `.lsi`, `.inp`,
`.bdf`), and material-card libraries are file-payloads with strong version
needs (a typical study sweeps a parameter and produces N decks per run). The
existing `FileReference` + `aidocs/data/46-payload-versioning-design.md` shape
handles this; what's *missing* is a **typed annotation on the predecessor edge**
to mark "this deck variant is the parameter-sweep child of that one" rather
than a free-form predecessor link. This is the same shape the MFFD rework-loop
case wants (see CLAUDE.md "Industrial Manufacturing & Quality Engineer" role).

### (b) Result-set ingestion — already covered by VIS-F1, validate against this cluster

`aidocs/16` row VIS-F1 (FEM-results plugin, `shepard-plugin-vis-fem`) already
designs for `.odb` / `.rst` / `.op2` / `.med` / `.h3d` / `.vtu` / `.xdmf` /
`.pvd`. The acceptance test cited there is an Abaqus `.odb` of a CF/LMPAEK
ultrasonic-weld T-joint. **The Toso cluster's typical result file is an
LS-DYNA d3plot family (`d3plot`, `d3plot01`, …, `binout`)** — these are not
on the VIS-F1 format matrix. Either VIS-F1 grows a d3plot ingester (LS-PrePost
batch convert, or `pyDyna` / `qd-cae-python` open-source readers) or the design
needs a sibling `shepard-plugin-vis-fem-lsdyna`. **Recommend updating VIS-F1
acceptance test to include a d3plot case** before the plugin lands.

### (c) Parameter-sweep tracking — `Activity` per run, but no sweep-parent primitive

LS-DYNA parameter sweeps (e.g. 50 decks varying material-card strain-rate
constants for a sensitivity study) produce 50 result sets. The PROV1a `Activity`
shape captures each individual run, but there is no first-class concept of
**"sweep set"** that groups N runs as one logical investigation. Options:

1. A Collection containing 50 DataObjects, one per run (works today; loses the
   semantic of "these are a coordinated sweep, not 50 independent objects").
2. A new `:Sweep` node `[:CONSISTS_OF]→ Activity` with a sweep-parameter
   manifest (parameter name, range, units). Stronger semantically; new entity.
3. A typed predecessor edge `[:SWEEP_SIBLING_OF]` connecting the 50 Activities
   peer-to-peer. Lighter; less obviously queryable.

Option 1 ships today; option 2 should appear in a future revision of
`aidocs/data/50-experiment-orchestration.md` once a real cluster sweep is
ingested. This is *not* a blocker for first-contact engagement — the cluster
will be happy with Collection-grouped runs as a starting point.

### (d) Experiment-to-simulation validation pairs — Predecessor edge can carry this

Every Toso-cluster publication is fundamentally a **sim-vs-test validation
story**. The model needs to express: "this Abaqus result was validated against
that drop-tower test." Today the predecessor/successor edge can encode the
relation, but there is no typed `[:VALIDATES]` or `[:VALIDATED_BY]` edge
distinct from "is a derivative of". The `aidocs/data/81-spatial-data-binding.md`
design already proposes `[:VALIDATES]` for the spatial-data binding case — the
**same predicate should generalise to sim-vs-test pairs**, not be scoped only
to spatial bindings. This is a small, free design refinement at the SB1 level.

### (e) Mesh + boundary-condition annotation — fits the FEM annotator umbrella

CAD1's `GeometryAnnotation` model already allows "FEM node-set" and
"FEM element-set" annotations (`aidocs/data/78-cad-geometry-annotator.md`). To
serve the Toso cluster, this needs to extend to **boundary-condition
annotations** (a set of nodes carrying a prescribed displacement / constraint /
contact-surface ID) and **material-zone annotations** (a region carrying a
material-card reference). Both are natural extensions of the existing
annotation model — they fit as additional annotation-kinds, no schema upheaval.

## 5. Showcase opportunity — smallest viable Toso-cluster demo

The cluster's headline 2024–2025 narrative is **LH₂ fuselage-tank
crashworthiness** (eLib 221271, 215571, 213377, 204351). This is precisely the
shape of demo that would resonate with both Clean Aviation JU funding bodies
and DLR-internal stakeholders.

**Smallest viable showcase ("LH₂ tank crush — sim vs test"), shippable to
a v6+ release demo collection:**

- **1 CAD geometry**: an LH₂-tank-mount STEP or glTF (anonymised /
  synthesised — real geometry stays DLR IP) → renders via VIS-C1
- **3 material cards**: CF/PEEK skin, Ti-6Al-4V liner, foam-core liner attach
  → as File payloads with `Material` annotation kind
- **1 LS-DYNA input deck**: drop-test impact scenario → File payload, versioned
- **1 d3plot result family**: ~20 timesteps, von-Mises field, deformed shape
  → via VIS-F1 (after the d3plot ingester is added per §4(b))
- **1 companion drop-tower test**: high-speed video + 4 timeseries channels
  (impact-mass accel, ground-reaction force, two strain gauges) → existing
  Timeseries + Video payloads
- **1 typed `[:VALIDATES]` edge** from the sim result to the test result
- **1 narrative annotation** capturing the "predicted peak g vs measured peak g"
  comparison

Total: ~10 DataObjects, ~15 payloads, ~50 MB of assets — comfortably in the
seed-script class alongside `examples/lumen-showcase` and `examples/mffd-showcase`.

The narrative arc reads: **"shepard captured one campaign's test + sim
artefacts as a connected dataset, surfaced the validation gap, and made the
material-card → input-deck → d3plot → drop-test chain traversable in one UI
session."** That is the elevator pitch a Clean Aviation JU programme manager
would understand inside 90 seconds.

The cluster's secondary candidate showcase is **AFP-defect impact simulation**
(eLib 214898, 207501, 210705 — Yoo + Vinot + Toso). This pairs naturally with
the existing `examples/mffd-showcase` (which already covers the AFP process
side), so a Toso-cluster contribution there would be a **simulation overlay on
the existing MFFD demo** rather than a new standalone showcase. Recommend this
as the *second* engagement after the LH₂ standalone proves the shape works.
